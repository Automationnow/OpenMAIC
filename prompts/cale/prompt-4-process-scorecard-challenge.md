# Prompt 4: The Process Scorecard Challenge

**CALE Module:** Module 2 — Process Candidate Evaluation  
**Objective:** Test the candidate's ability to accurately score and debate process feasibility using the CALE methodology.

---

## OpenMAIC Prompt

```
Let's run a Process Candidate Scorecard exercise. Present a detailed, ambiguous business process (e.g., employee onboarding, vendor invoice dispute resolution, or customer churn prediction). Describe the volume, the rules involved, the error rates, and the systems used. I will attempt to score this process out of 25 points using the CALE methodology. After I provide my score and rationale, Agent 1 (The Optimist) should argue why the score should be higher, highlighting the strategic value. Agent 2 (The Realist) should argue why the score should be lower, pointing out system compatibility issues. Force me to defend my final score.
```

---

## Agent Roles

| Agent | Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|---|
| Agent 1 | The Optimist | Argues the score should be higher — emphasizes strategic value and opportunity |
| Agent 2 | The Realist | Argues the score should be lower — highlights system compatibility and feasibility issues |
| You | The CALE Candidate | Score the process out of 25 using the CALE methodology and defend your rationale |

---

## Scoring Framework (CALE Process Candidate Scorecard — 25 Points)

| Dimension | Max Points |
|---|---|
| Process Volume & Frequency | 5 |
| Rule Clarity & Standardization | 5 |
| Data Quality & Availability | 5 |
| System Accessibility & Stability | 5 |
| Strategic & Business Value | 5 |
| **Total** | **25** |

---

## Learning Outcomes

- Participants can apply the CALE Process Candidate Scorecard methodology accurately
- Participants can defend a scoring decision under challenge from multiple perspectives
- Participants understand the difference between strategic value and technical feasibility in process selection
