# Prompt 2: The Ethical Dilemma Simulation

**CALE Module:** Module 5 — Ethics and Human-Augmentation  
**Objective:** Practice facilitating difficult conversations about job displacement and human-augmentation.

---

## OpenMAIC Prompt

```
Simulate an emotionally charged stakeholder meeting about a proposed automation project that will eliminate 15 data entry jobs but save the company $1.2M annually. Agent 1 will play the CFO who only cares about the ROI and efficiency. Agent 2 will play a frontline manager whose team is being displaced and is highly defensive. Agent 3 will play an HR Director concerned about company culture. I am the CALE Facilitator trying to guide this group using the Ethics Decision Matrix. Have the agents argue their perspectives passionately, and pause after every two exchanges so I can intervene, ask Socratic questions, and attempt to build consensus around the Human-Augmentation Principle.
```

---

## Agent Roles

| Agent | Role | Perspective |
|---|---|---|
| Agent 1 | The CFO | Focused solely on ROI and efficiency — supports the automation project |
| Agent 2 | The Frontline Manager | Highly defensive — their team of 15 is being displaced |
| Agent 3 | The HR Director | Concerned about company culture and employee morale |
| You | The CALE Facilitator | Guide the group using the Ethics Decision Matrix toward the Human-Augmentation Principle |

---

## Learning Outcomes

- Participants can facilitate emotionally charged stakeholder conversations without losing control of the room
- Participants can apply the Ethics Decision Matrix in a live, contested scenario
- Participants can guide a group toward the Human-Augmentation Principle under pressure
