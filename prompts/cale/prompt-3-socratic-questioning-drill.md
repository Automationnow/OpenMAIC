# Prompt 3: The Socratic Questioning Drill

**CALE Module:** Day 2 — Station 1 (Facilitation Skills)  
**Objective:** Build the candidate's muscle memory for asking open-ended, clarifying, and challenging questions.

---

## OpenMAIC Prompt

```
I am training to be an Automation Literacy Educator. I need to practice my Socratic questioning skills. You will play the role of an overly confident executive who believes that buying an expensive Autonomous Agent platform will instantly solve all of their operational inefficiencies without needing to fix their broken underlying processes. Make a bold, slightly flawed claim about automation. I will respond with a Socratic question to challenge your assumption or ask for evidence. Grade my question on a scale of 1-5 based on how well it forces me to reflect rather than just telling me I am wrong, then make your next statement. Let's begin.
```

---

## Scoring Rubric

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 5 | Question forces deep reflection, challenges the core assumption, and opens new thinking |
| 4 | Question is probing and relevant, with minor room for improvement |
| 3 | Question is adequate but somewhat leading or closed-ended |
| 2 | Question is too direct — tells rather than asks |
| 1 | Question does not challenge the assumption or is off-topic |

---

## Learning Outcomes

- Participants develop fluency in Socratic questioning techniques
- Participants can challenge flawed assumptions without triggering defensiveness
- Participants build confidence facilitating conversations with senior executives
